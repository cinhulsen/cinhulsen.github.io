Grill Night

One night, one big cookout. Feeds 8. Simple prep, massive payoff.

Main · Beef
Reverse-Sear Ribeyes
Low effort ~90 min total Shared with group
Season night before

Both cuts are intentional — cook them together and put everything out mixed on a platter family-style. Ribeyes for richness, strips for a leaner bite. Everyone gets some of each. If bone-in ribeyes, budget for ~20% loss to the bone, so lean toward the higher end of the range.

What you need
Ribeyes — 4–5 lbs (boneless preferred; bone-in or cowboy cut if unavailable) NY strips or sirloins — 4–5 lbs (porterhouse or T-bone if unavailable) Kosher salt Coarse black pepper Garlic powder Herb compound butter
Steps
1
Night before: season steaks heavily with salt, pepper, garlic powder. Leave uncovered in fridge overnight.
2
Day of: pull steaks out 30–45 min before cooking to come to room temp.
3
Bake at 250°F on a wire rack until internal temp hits 115°F (medium-rare target) — about 25–35 min.
4
Crank grill to high heat. Sear steaks 60–90 seconds per side until you get a deep crust.
5
Rest 5–8 min, then top each with a pat of compound butter before slicing.
Main · Chicken Tacos
Grilled Chicken Thigh Tacos
Low effort ~30 min grill Feeds 8
Season night before

Boneless thighs are the move for tacos — they stay juicy, cook fast, and chop up easily to load into tortillas. Simple taco rub, no marinade needed. Toppings do the rest of the work.

Taco rub
Chili powder Cumin Garlic powder Smoked paprika Onion powder Dried oregano Kosher salt
Toppings
Flour or corn tortillas Packaged pico de gallo Store-bought guacamole Shredded cabbage (bag) Cotija cheese Sour cream Lime wedges Fresh cilantro Hot sauce (Cholula or Valentina) Fresh jalapeño (optional)
Steps
1
Thighs are pre-rubbed from the night before. Pull from the fridge when you start the grill — they're ready to go.
2
Grill over medium-high heat 6–7 min per side until internal temp hits 165°F. These cook fast — don't walk away.
3
Rest 5 min, then chop or slice into bite-sized pieces.
4
Warm tortillas on the grill for 30 seconds per side. Set out all toppings and let guys build their own.
Side · Grill
Crispy Smashed Potatoes
Easy 35 min
Prep night before

Boil until tender, smash flat, hit them with olive oil and seasoning, then finish on the grill for crispy edges and a charred crust. Way better than roasted potatoes and they cook alongside everything else.

What you need
2 lbs baby potatoes Olive oil Kosher salt Garlic powder Coarse black pepper Fresh rosemary (optional) Sour cream (for serving)
Steps
1
Night before: boil baby potatoes in heavily salted water until fork-tender, about 20 min. Drain, steam dry 5 min, smash flat on a sheet pan, brush with olive oil, season, cover, and refrigerate overnight.
2
When ready to cook: pull sheet pan straight from the fridge to the grill — already oiled and seasoned. The cold helps them crisp up even better.
3
Grill over medium-high heat 4–5 min per side until crispy and charred at the edges. Don't move them — let the crust form.
5
Serve hot with sour cream on the side. Optional: hit with fresh rosemary or a drizzle of chimichurri.
Side · Skillet
Street Corn Salad (Esquites)
Zero effort 15 min

Same elote flavors — mayo, cotija, chili powder, lime — but made with frozen corn charred in a skillet. No husks, no cobs, easier to serve to 8 guys. Cooks on the stovetop while the grill is busy.

What you need
Frozen corn (3 bags, ~16 oz each) Mayonnaise Cotija cheese (or parmesan) Chili powder Limes Olive oil Kosher salt
Steps
1
Heat a large cast iron or heavy skillet over high heat until very hot. Add a thin layer of oil.
2
Add frozen corn in a single layer (work in batches if needed). Don't stir — let it sit and char for 2–3 min, then toss. You want some real color on it.
3
Season with salt. Remove from heat and let cool slightly.
4
Toss with mayo, cotija, and chili powder. Squeeze lime over the top. Serve in the skillet or a bowl.

Things to Bring

Pack these from home — don't buy at the store.

🎵 Bluetooth speaker
Airbnb rarely has a good one. One designated speaker for the patio/grill area makes a big difference.
🃏 Cards & games
A deck of cards, poker chips, or whatever the group plays. Something for the nights you're not going out.
💊 Ibuprofen / Tylenol
Someone will need it. Don't make a pharmacy run on day 2.
🧴 Sunscreen
4 days on the course adds up fast. Buy a big bottle and leave it in the car for the week.
🍺 Beer, liquor & mixers
Coordinate in the group chat before the trip — styles, quantities, who's handling it. Split the cost separately from food. Designate one or two guys to make the run.
💧 Sparkling water (case)
LaCroix, Topo Chico, etc. Good for guys who want something besides beer, and doubles as a mixer.
🧊 Ice
Confirm if the Airbnb has an ice maker. If not, grab 3 bags on arrival — you'll go through more than you think with a cooler and 8 guys.
🏌️ Golf gear checklist
Clubs, shoes, glove, range finder, tees, ball markers, extra balls. Pack the night before — not the morning of.

Prep Timeline

What to knock out early so grill night is smooth and low-stress.

Night Before
Before You Go to Bed
~40 min Biggest payoff per minute
1
Season the steaks. Pat dry, then hit generously on all sides with kosher salt, coarse pepper, and garlic powder. Place on a wire rack or plate, uncovered, in the fridge overnight. The dry brine is the single biggest upgrade to the crust and flavor — don't skip it.
2
Rub the chicken thighs. Mix the taco rub spices together and coat the thighs on all sides. Put in a zip-lock bag or covered bowl and refrigerate. More time in the rub = more flavor.
3
Boil and smash the potatoes. Boil baby potatoes until fork-tender (~20 min), drain, steam dry 5 min, then smash flat on a sheet pan. Brush with olive oil, season with salt, garlic powder, and pepper, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate. They'll go straight from the fridge to the grill the next day — and the cold fridge actually helps them crisp up better.
4
Make compound butter (if not store-bought). Soften butter, mix in herbs or garlic, roll in plastic wrap, and refrigerate. Ready to slice and drop on steaks right off the grill.
Day Of · Afternoon
About 1 Hour Before Eating
~10 min
1
Set up the taco topping station. Open and portion the pico, guac, cabbage, cotija, and sour cream into bowls or containers. Cover and refrigerate. Slice limes and set aside. Pull it all out 30 min before eating and it's ready to go.
Day Of · Pre-Cook
About 1 Hour Before
~20 min
1
Char the corn for esquites. Heat a cast iron skillet on high, char the frozen corn in batches until you get real color. Let cool slightly, then toss with mayo, cotija, chili powder, and lime. Store in a bowl — serve at room temp or slightly warm. Done well before the grill is on.
2
Pull steaks from the fridge. Set them out at room temp 30–45 min before cooking. Cold steaks cook unevenly.
3
Preheat the oven to 250°F for the reverse sear. Get the grill going — you'll want it screaming hot for the final sear.
Cook Time
When You're Ready to Eat
~45 min active

If the above is done, cook night is mostly grill watching and timing. See the full timing rundown on the Grill Night tab.

1
Steaks go into the 250°F oven — ~30 min to reach 115°F internal.
2
Chicken on the grill over medium-high — 6–7 min per side. Heat beans and mac & cheese while the grill is busy.
3
Pull chicken, crank grill to high. Sear steaks 60–90 sec per side. Grill smashed potatoes alongside.
4
Rest steaks 5–8 min, top with compound butter, slice and plate everything family-style. Warm tortillas last.

Trip Tips

Things that'll save you headaches.

Confirm grill type before you go
Message the Airbnb host ahead of time — charcoal vs propane changes what you bring. If it's charcoal, grab a chimney starter and lighter. Propane, just confirm the tank has fuel.
Do one big grocery run on arrival
Don't split the shopping. One guy + one other person, hit the store together the day you arrive. Everything on the list, one shot. Put it all on one card and split the total equally 8 ways after — easiest way to handle it.
Season the steaks before bed
Overnight dry brine (just salt) is genuinely the biggest upgrade to a steak with zero effort. Crust development and flavor are noticeably better the next day.
Check for an ice maker
Airbnb fridges often have them. If so, skip buying ice bags. If not, grab 3 bags on arrival — you'll go through more than you think with 8 guys and a cooler.
Split the booze cost separately
Coordinate beer and liquor preferences in the group chat before the trip. Designate one or two guys to handle it and split the cost separately from food — it avoids everyone's preferences being ignored.
Premade sides are not cheating
Costco mac & cheese tray, jarred queso, baked beans from a can — you're on a golf trip, not competing on a cooking show. The goal is good food with minimal cleanup. Lean into it.
Thermometer is the only rule
Bring a cheap instant-read thermometer. Doesn't matter how experienced the grill guy is — with 8 steaks at different thicknesses and temperatures, guessing by feel is how you ruin someone's dinner.
